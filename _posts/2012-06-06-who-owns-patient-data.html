The notion of ownership is inadequate for health information.For instance, no one has an absolute right to destroy health information.But we all understand what it means to own an automobile: You can drive the car you own into a tree or into the ocean if you want to.No one has the legal right to do things like that to a master copy of health information.All of the groups above have a complex series of rights and responsibilities relating to health information that should never be trivialized into ownership.
